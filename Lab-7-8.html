#Function definitions
def expected_sigma(num_higgs_events,num_qcd_events,L_higgs=len(higgs.mass),L_qcd=len(qcd.mass)):
    N_higgs = (num_higgs_events/L_higgs)*100
    N_qcd = (num_qcd_events/L_qcd)*20000
    return N_higgs/np.sqrt(N_qcd)

def make_cuts(lower_limit,upper_limit,parameter,qcd_data=qcd.copy(),higgs_data=higgs.copy()):
    qcd_cuts = []
    higgs_cuts = []
    for i in range(len(qcd_data[parameter])):
        if qcd_data[parameter][i] >= upper_limit or qcd_data[parameter][i] <= lower_limit:
            qcd_cuts.append(i)
        if higgs_data[parameter][i] >= upper_limit or higgs_data[parameter][i] <= lower_limit:
            higgs_cuts.append(i)
    qcd_data = qcd_data.drop(qcd_cuts).reset_index()
    qcd_data.pop('index')
    higgs_data = higgs_data.drop(higgs_cuts).reset_index()
    higgs_data.pop('index')
    return qcd_data,higgs_data

def cut_data(lower_limit,upper_limit,parameter,data):
    data_cuts = []
    for i in range(len(data[parameter])):
        if data[parameter][i] >= upper_limit or data[parameter][i] <= lower_limit:
            data_cuts.append(i)
    data = data.drop(data_cuts).reset_index()
    data.pop('index')
    return data

poisson = stats.poisson(20000)
sigma = stats.norm.isf(poisson.sf(20100))
expected = expected_sigma(100000,100000)

Our data sets are within the $p_T$ range of 250-500 GeV/c with expected yields of $N_{Higgs} = 100$ and $N_{QCD}=20000$. Supposing these yields are Poisson-distributed, we can calculate the significance of our Higgs signal as usual by integrating the PMF of the QCD distribution ($\lambda = 20000$) from $20100$ to $\infty$ and mapping the probability to a standard Gaussian. Doing this we get that the significance of our signal is {{np.round(sigma,3)}}.

We can also approximate the significance of our signal as $\frac{N_{Higgs}}{\sqrt{N_{QCD}}}$. Without any event selection we have $\frac{N_{Higgs}}{\sqrt{N_{QCD}}} = $ {{np.round(expected,3)}}. This method will be helpful as our distribution will change when we make cuts to our data. The expected yields will also change as $N^*_X = \left(1 - \frac{n}{100000}\right)N_X$ where $n$ is the amount of data points cut from the set.

7.2¶

The logical choice for making mass cuts is to keep the Higgs signal data around ~125 GeV/c$^2$ and cut the QCD background data in that same range. The maximum mass in our Higgs signal dataset is ~142 GeV/c^$2$, so we started from 120-142 GeV/c$^2$ and worked our way closer to 125 GeV/c$^2$ from the lower limit only since we want to preserve as much of the Higgs data as possible. We've tabulated the results below.
